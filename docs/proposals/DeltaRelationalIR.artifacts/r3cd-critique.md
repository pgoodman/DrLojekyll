# R3c-ii+R3d plan critique (Fable)

VERDICT: REVISE BEFORE IMPLEMENTATION — architecturally right, not implementable as written. The plan gets the big calls correct: R3c-ii+R3d as one suite-gated atomic unit (F9(i)'s disposition realized in intent), GROUP_UPDATE as the first sole-authority payload-consuming family (the exact F1 go-forward shape, correctly needing no V-PRED-XCHECK), the strict sign-independent ready_after lift riding the existing monotone fixpoint (verified sound against the agg-feeding-negate and agg-over-agg attacks — average_weight's double nesting genuinely falls out of the crossover/branch lifts reading the lifted drain_stratum), reuse of the entire acyclic claim/frontier/commit tail, and oracle-blessed goldens. But it fails its own airtightness standard in five load-bearing places: (1) the emit_touched one-net-pair contract is wrong at group birth/death — identity-initialized sealed values mean every first-touch retracts a phantom row and every group-death asserts one, aborting the commit sweep on batch 1 of every aggregate case; the fix (per-group occupancy) amends the BINDING statecell spec and the landed R3a header. (2) §4's codegen story fabricates compiler-known reduction semantics ('bodies emitted from the declared shape') that a #functor declaration cannot supply; the real dependency is the never-written functor ABI (AggregatingFunctors §3's init/fold/unfold/merge/emit driver contract) plus threading functors into the landed StateCellStore API — the largest un-owned decision in the plan. (3) V-ALGEBRA's gating mechanism is broken (the num_errors->nullopt gate runs before BuildDRInventory; Main.cpp's success-path assert turns the diag into exit 134, not exit 1) — move it into the pre-pass slot the F14 rows vacate. (4) Whole never-executed subsystems (BuildDataModel table-forcing for aggregate inputs, eager-walk termination at the aggregate boundary, SuffixesOf chain-breaking so no bogus seed fold double-derives the agg table, linearizer band keys for the two new op kinds — without which V-BAND-HAZARD aborts every aggregate compile) have no diffs at all. (5) The runall re-staging blesses before the dispatch edits (producing no goldens to bless), and the kvindex_1-4 flip predictions contradict the ratified kv-undeclared reject unless the four test .dr files are also annotated — plus the four stale pre-ADL drivers must be rewritten, and the oracle needs MAP functor implementations (div_i32/add_i32) or both flagship sidecars are impossible. F9's three hazards score: (i) golden gap — right disposition, broken recipe; (ii) V-ALGEBRA — in the unit, wrong mechanism; (iii) lift rule — sound core, but the plan overstates validator coverage (V-READY provably does NOT catch WRONG-A: equal strata pass its <=) and omits the monotone/induction-owned/table-less input preconditions, including the missing can_produce_deletions on QueryAggregateImpl. TO THE OWNER before implementation, explicitly: (a) the aggregate/merge functor driver ABI (blocking); (b) the occupancy amendment to the binding statecell spec; (c) reject-vs-support for aggregates over monotone/induction-owned inputs; (d) ratify the V-ALGEBRA layer (recommend: the vacated pre-pass slot); (e) the STATE_SEAL ordering pin (concur with the plan's recommendation). Predictions are otherwise falsifiable and the corpus annotations match the ratified policy for data/examples; the honest headline risk the plan itself names — the lift with no old lift to cross-check — is real but smaller than the five gaps above, and its true safety net is the .batches oracle cases, which should be extended to at least one of the flipped kvindex/aggregate corner shapes.

## [CRITICAL] The emit_touched one-net-pair contract is wrong at group BIRTH and group DEATH, and neither the plan, the binding statecell spec, nor the landed StateCell.h can express the fix. The plan's guard `if new != old: UPDATECOUNT(-, (g++old)); UPDATECOUNT(+, (g++new))` retracts a never-present row on a group's first touch (old = the identity-initialized sealed value) and asserts a row for an emptied group on death. First batch of EVERY aggregate case is all births, so C_nr goes 0->-1 on the phantom (g,identity) row and the commit sweep's >=0-per-class assert aborts (exit 134) on batch 1 — nothing in the unit can go green. Death is worse: value-based suppression is impossible (SUM=0 is ambiguous between an empty group and a zero-sum group), so the fix requires per-group OCCUPANCY (working member count + sealed presence) and a generalized pair guard (- iff old-present, + iff new-present, pair iff both and new!=old). Recompute::Emit over an all-dead membership (ReduceLive with every count 0) is likewise undefined.

EVIDENCE: StateCell.h:265-273 — FindOrAddGroup does `sealed.Add(Algebra::SealFrom(w))` on the identity working, so Old() of a first-touch group is the identity's Emit; no occupancy field exists anywhere in the store (grep). StateCell.h:167-180 Recompute::Fold/Emit track signed counts but ReduceLive's empty-live case is unspecified. v3-spec-statecell.md §2.2 (ln388-399) and both §6 hand-traces (ln680-712) only ever trace pre-existing groups; V-ONE-NET-PAIR (ln493-497) never states the birth/death cases. CLAUDE.md invariant: 'the commit sweep asserts both >= 0 per class'.

RECOMMENDATION: Before implementation: amend the binding v3-spec-statecell §2.2/§2.5 (V-ONE-NET-PAIR) with the occupancy-generalized guard, extend StateCell.h (R3a follow-up, unit-tested birth/death/zero-sum-nonempty traces), and record the binding-spec deviation in the ledger per the epoch's own F8a discipline. This is an owner-visible amendment to a ratified binding spec.

## [CRITICAL] The codegen/runtime binding (§4) is unimplementable as written and hides the largest un-owned decision: WHO SUPPLIES the reduction bodies. The plan claims 'Reduce_<functor> is a generated policy struct ... bodies emitted from the functor's declared shape (sum_i32: Fold=w.sum+=sign*v)' — but a #functor declaration carries only types, not semantics (sum and max are shape-identical), and the design doc says the FUNCTOR supplies init/fold/unfold/merge/emit — a driver-facing ABI that was explicitly deferred ('the functor-ABI one-pager') and never written. Either functors get threaded through the store calls (API change to R3a's landed header, and Seal() runs in the commit band where functors must be in scope) or the ABI makes aggregate policies driver-supplied types — both are generated-surface (driver-facing) decisions.

EVIDENCE: AggregatingFunctors.md §3 (ln84-86): 'the functor supplies init/fold/unfold/merge/emit over a state blob whose layout it declares'; ln124-126: the functor-ABI one-pager listed as future 'cheap early work'. RECOMMENDATION: NAME THIS TO THE OWNER before implementation: the aggregate/merge functor calling convention (DatabaseFunctors member shape, how @invertible's unfold is supplied, whether corpus reductions are engine-builtin-by-name as the oracle will do, and how functors reach StateCell Fold/Emit/Seal).
